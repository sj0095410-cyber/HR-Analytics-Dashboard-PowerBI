# HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI

## Project Overview

This project is an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built using Microsoft Power BI. The dashboard helps HR teams analyze employee attrition patterns and identify factors affecting workforce retention.

The dashboard provides insights into employee demographics, job roles, salary ranges, job satisfaction levels, and department-wise attrition.

---

## Dashboard Features

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* Total Employees: 1,480
* Average Age: 36.92 Years
* Attrition Count: 238
* Attrition Rate: 16.08%
* Average Monthly Income: 7K
* Active Employees: 1K

---

## Dashboard Visualizations

### Attrition by Department

Analyzes employee attrition across departments such as:

* Research & Development
* Sales
* Human Resources

### Attrition by Job Role

Displays attrition counts for various job roles including:

* Laboratory Technician
* Sales Executive
* Research Scientist
* Sales Representative
* Human Resources
* Manager

### Attrition by Job Satisfaction

Shows the relationship between employee satisfaction levels and attrition.

### Attrition by Salary Slab

Analyzes attrition across salary ranges:

* Up to 5K
* 5K–10K
* 10K–15K
* 15K+

### Attrition by Gender

Visualizes attrition distribution between male and female employees.

### Attrition by Age Group

Breaks down attrition by age categories:

* 18–25
* 26–35
* 36–45
* 46–55
* 55+

## Interactive Filters

The dashboard includes slicers for:

* Department
* Gender

These filters allow users to dynamically explore attrition trends across different employee groups.

## Key Insights

* Research & Development department shows the highest attrition.
* Employees aged 26–35 contribute the largest share of attrition.
* Lower salary ranges experience significantly higher attrition.
* Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives have the highest attrition counts.
* Attrition patterns vary across job satisfaction levels and gender groups.
